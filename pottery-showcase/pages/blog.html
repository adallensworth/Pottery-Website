New Glazing Techniques I'm Exploring

June 15, 2023

I've been experimenting with some exciting new glazing techniques that create unique textures and colors. In this post, I'll share my process and the results of my recent experiments with crystalline glazes.

Crystalline glazes have always fascinated me with their depth and complexity. Unlike traditional glazes that form a uniform surface, crystalline glazes develop visible crystal formations during the firing process, creating stunning patterns that resemble frost, flowers, or galaxies.

The process begins with a carefully formulated glaze recipe containing zinc oxide and silica, which are the primary components that form the crystals. The firing schedule is crucial - after reaching peak temperature (usually around 2300Â°F), the kiln is cooled to a specific temperature and held there for several hours, allowing the crystals to grow.

What makes this technique particularly challenging is that each piece requires individual attention during firing. The glaze runs significantly, so each pot needs to be placed on a pedestal with a catch basin to collect the excess glaze.

Behind the Scenes: My Studio Setup

May 28, 2023

Many people ask about my workspace. Here's a look at how I've organized my pottery studio to maximize creativity and efficiency.

Creating a functional pottery studio requires careful planning and organization. After years of refining my space, I've developed a layout that supports my creative process while maintaining efficiency.

The studio is divided into distinct zones: clay preparation, throwing, handbuilding, glazing, and firing. Each area is equipped with the necessary tools and materials, minimizing the need to move around too much during focused work sessions.

Finding Inspiration in Nature

April 12, 2023

Nature has always been my greatest source of inspiration. In this post, I explore how elements from the natural world influence my pottery designs.

From the spiral patterns of shells to the textures of tree bark, nature provides an endless source of design inspiration. Recently, I've been particularly drawn to the organic forms of river stones and how water shapes them over time.

This influence can be seen in my latest collection, where I've incorporated smooth, rounded forms with subtle variations in texture and color. The glazes I've developed for this series mimic the earthy tones and mineral-like qualities found in natural stone formations.
